## Top Stories

**EU Forces Google to Open Android and Share Search Data With AI Competitors**

The European Commission issued binding requirements under the Digital Markets Act ordering Google to make Android accessible to rival AI assistants and share search data with competitors, including AI developers. Third-party assistants will gain voice activation and cross-app capabilities across 11 Android feature groups starting July 2027, while anonymized search data (ranking, query, click, and view data) must be shared on fair terms beginning January 2027. This directly undermines Google's two strongest moats: default placement on 2 billion Android devices and decades of search behavior data no competitor can replicate. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

**Gemini 3.5 Pro Delayed Again as Google Scrambles**

Google's flagship Gemini 3.5 Pro reportedly missed its July 17 target, marking the third delay for the model. The company is now exploring a stopgap Gemini 3.6 Flash release to put something in market while the Pro model gets fixed. Google has published no official model card, pricing, or benchmarks, leaving all claims unconfirmed. Alphabet shares fell approximately 4% on the delay reports. The timing is particularly damaging given the concurrent EU regulatory action targeting Google's distribution advantages. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

**Kimi K3 Tops Coding Leaderboard, Free Weights Coming July 27**

Moonshot AI's Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion-parameter open model, took the top spot on a major coding leaderboard, beating Claude Fable 5 and triggering fresh debate about the US-China AI rivalry. The model will release open weights on July 27, combining benchmark leadership with free access. While K3 ranks around ninth on general chat tasks, its specialist coding performance at no per-token cost represents a direct challenge to paid frontier APIs for high-volume coding workloads. ## Business & Infrastructure

**Oracle Cuts 30,000 Jobs to Fund Stargate**

Oracle is cutting up to 30,000 employees (roughly 18% of its global workforce) in the largest workforce reduction in company history, freeing an estimated $8-10 billion in annual cash flow for AI infrastructure investments. The cuts fund Oracle's role in Stargate, the $500 billion AI infrastructure initiative with OpenAI and SoftBank, anchored by a $300 billion five-year cloud contract with OpenAI expected to generate approximately $30 billion annually from 4.5 gigawatts of Oracle-built data center capacity. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

**SAP Invests Over €1 Billion in Prior Labs**

SAP completed its acquisition of Prior Labs and committed to investing more than €1 billion over four years to scale the Freiburg-based startup into a globally leading frontier AI lab. Prior Labs, founded by Frank Hutter, Noah Hollmann, and Sauraj Gambhir roughly 18 months ago, built the TabPFN model series for tabular foundation models that was published in Nature and set state-of-the-art benchmarks. The lab will continue operating independently. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

## Products & Tools

**Microsoft's Project Perception Targets AI Security**

Microsoft is preparing Project Perception, an AI cybersecurity platform that finds and fixes software vulnerabilities using models from Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic together, positioned as a lower-cost alternative to Anthropic's Mythos-class security offering. The system examines a company's code, cloud infrastructure, and endpoints to identify exploitable weaknesses, explain their impact, and propose fixes. Microsoft has not publicly confirmed availability, pricing, or customer eligibility. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

**Alibaba Claims Qwen3.8 Trails Only Fable 5**

## Research & Safety

**AI Radiology Models Confidently Wrong on Diagnosis**

A new benchmark called RadLE 2.0 found AI models frequently deliver incorrect radiology findings with full confidence, reading X-rays and producing wrong diagnoses without signaling uncertainty. The "confidently wrong" failure mode poses specific clinical risks because hedged incorrect answers prompt second opinions while confident incorrect answers do not. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

**AI Text Detectors Fail Against Style Imitation**

Recent testing shows AI text detectors fail when models are instructed to imitate a specific writer's style, undermining their reliability for academic and content verification use cases. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

## Policy & Regulation

**Xi Jinping Calls for AI Emergency Response Systems**

Chinese President Xi Jinping, delivering his first-ever keynote at the 2026 World AI Conference in Shanghai, called for emergency response systems to keep AI in check. The conference, which closes today after four days, included the launch of the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO) with 29 founding countries including Pakistan, Russia, and Kazakhstan. The conference ran more than 140 forums with over 1,100 exhibitors. **Google's AI Search Threatens Open Web**

The New York Times reports on concerns that Google's AI Mode, which replaces traditional search results with conversational responses from its Gemini chatbot, is imperiling the open web by removing traffic to source websites. The shift from hyperlinked results to AI-generated summaries raises fundamental questions about content attribution and publisher sustainability.
