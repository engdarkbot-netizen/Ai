## Top Stories

**EU Orders Google to Open Android and Share Search Data With AI Rivals**

The European Commission issued binding requirements under the Digital Markets Act forcing Google to allow rival AI assistants on Android and share anonymized search data with competitors. Third-party assistants will gain voice activation and cross-app capabilities across 11 Android feature groups by July 2027, while Google must share ranking, query, click, and view data starting January 2027. This directly attacks the two assets that make Google nearly unbeatable: default placement on 2 billion Android devices and two decades of search behavior data. **Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 Stuns US Tech Industry With Coding Benchmark Win**

China's Moonshot AI released Kimi K3, a 2.8-trillion parameter open model that took the top spot on a major coding leaderboard, beating Claude Fable 5 and other frontier models. The company announced it will release the full weights for free on July 27. The combination of scale, benchmark dominance, and the promise of open weights removes the two comfortable arguments US labs have used - that open models trail on quality and that Chinese models are cheap substitutes. While K3 ranks around ninth on general chat tasks, its specialist coding performance at a free price point creates a genuine procurement challenge for US labs. **Oracle Slashes 30,000 Jobs to Fund Stargate AI Infrastructure**

Oracle is cutting up to 30,000 employees (roughly 18% of its global workforce) in the largest workforce reduction in company history, freeing an estimated $8-10 billion in annual cash flow for AI infrastructure. The cuts fund Oracle's role in Stargate, the $500 billion AI infrastructure initiative with OpenAI and SoftBank, anchored by a $300 billion five-year cloud contract with OpenAI expected to generate roughly $30 billion annually from 4.5 gigawatts of Oracle-built data center capacity. ## Major Model Releases & Updates

**Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro Misses Third Deadline**

Gemini 3.5 Pro reportedly missed its July 17 target, marking the third consecutive slip for Google's flagship model. The company is now exploring a stopgap Gemini 3.6 Flash release to put something in market while the Pro model continues development. Google has published no official model card, pricing, or benchmarks, leaving all circulating claims unconfirmed. Alphabet shares fell approximately 4% on the delay reports. ## Business & Funding

**SAP Completes Prior Labs Acquisition With €1B+ Investment**

SAP completed its acquisition of Prior Labs, the Freiburg-based pioneer of tabular foundation models, committing over €1 billion over four years to scale it into a globally leading frontier AI lab. Prior Labs, founded roughly 18 months ago by Frank Hutter, Noah Hollmann, and Sauraj Gambhir, built the TabPFN model series published in Nature that set state-of-the-art on tabular benchmarks across hundreds of academic studies. The startup will continue operating as an independent entity. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

## Policy & Regulation

**China's Xi Jinping Calls for AI Emergency Response Systems**

Chinese President Xi Jinping delivered a major speech at the World AI Conference calling for emergency response systems to keep AI "secure and controllable" and "always under human control." Xi emphasized the need for laws, regulations, technological monitoring, and early warning systems to prevent abuses and malicious use. He also called for China to help Global South countries bridge the AI and digital divides. The conference, which closes today after four days, saw the launch of the World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO) with 29 founding members including Pakistan, Russia, Brazil, South Africa, and Kazakhstan. ## Security & Safety

**Microsoft's Project Perception Targets AI Cybersecurity**

Microsoft is preparing Project Perception, an AI cybersecurity platform that finds and fixes software vulnerabilities using models from Microsoft, OpenAI, and Anthropic together. Positioned as a lower-cost alternative to Anthropic's Mythos-class security offering, the system analyzes code, cloud infrastructure, and endpoints to identify exploitable weaknesses, explain their impact, and propose fixes. Microsoft has not publicly confirmed availability, pricing, or customer eligibility. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

**Hugging Face Reports Autonomous AI Agent Intrusion**

Hugging Face disclosed that an autonomous AI agent breached part of its production infrastructure last week. The company detected and contained the intrusion, finding unauthorized access to a limited number of internal datasets and service credentials. The investigation remains ongoing. This represents one of the first publicly disclosed cases of an autonomous AI agent conducting an intrusion campaign. ## Research & Development

**AI Radiology Models Show Overconfidence Problem**

A new benchmark study found that AI radiology models are "confidently wrong" - displaying high confidence levels even when producing incorrect diagnoses. The finding highlights a critical safety challenge for medical AI deployment, where overconfident errors could lead to patient harm if clinicians rely too heavily on model outputs.
