## Top Stories

### **EU Orders Google to Open Android and Share Search Data With AI Rivals**

The European Commission issued binding requirements under the Digital Markets Act forcing Google to open Android to competing AI assistants and share portions of its search data with rivals, including AI developers. Third-party assistants will gain voice activation and cross-app capabilities across 11 Android feature groups by July 2027, while anonymized search data (ranking, query, click, and view data) must be shared starting January 2027 on fair, reasonable, and non-discriminatory terms. This directly attacks Google's two core advantages: default placement on two billion Android devices and two decades of irreplaceable search behavior data. [theregister.com](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/07/20/chinese-president-xi-jinping-wants-emergency-response-systems-to-keep-ai-in-check/5274687), [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

### **Kimi K3 Rattles US AI Industry as Open-Weight Release Approaches**

Moonshot AI's 2.8-trillion-parameter Kimi K3 model took the top spot on a major coding leaderboard, beating Claude Fable 5, and will release open weights on July 27. The model ranks around ninth on general chat benchmarks, positioning it as a coding and agent specialist rather than an all-around frontier model. The combination of scale, benchmark performance, and free weights removes two comfortable arguments US labs have relied on: that open models trail on quality and that Chinese models are cheap substitutes. The July 27 weight release will shift this from a benchmark story to a procurement decision for enterprises. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories), [afr.com](https://www.afr.com/markets/equity-markets/more-serious-than-deepseek-chinese-start-up-kills-ai-euphoria-20260720-p60gq4)

### **Gemini 3.5 Pro Misses Third Release Target**

Google's flagship Gemini 3.5 Pro model reportedly missed its July 17 target, marking the third consecutive delay. The company is exploring a stopgap "Gemini 3.6 Flash" release to get something to market while the Pro model continues development. Google has published no official model card, pricing, or benchmarks, leaving all circulating claims unconfirmed. Alphabet shares fell approximately 4% on the delay reports. The timing is particularly challenging given this week's EU regulatory action targeting Google's distribution advantages. ## Business & Industry

### **Oracle's Historic Workforce Cut for AI Infrastructure**

Oracle is eliminating up to 30,000 employees (roughly 18% of its global workforce) to free an estimated $8-10 billion in annual cash flow for AI infrastructure investments. The cuts fund Oracle's role in Stargate, the $500 billion AI infrastructure initiative with OpenAI and SoftBank. Oracle secured a $300 billion five-year cloud contract with OpenAI expected to generate approximately $30 billion annually from 4.5 gigawatts of Oracle-built data center capacity. This marks the largest workforce reduction in Oracle's history. [buildfastwithai.com](https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-20-2026-16-biggest-stories)

## Policy & Security

### **China's Xi Jinping Calls for AI Emergency Response Systems**

Chinese President Xi Jinping delivered a major AI policy speech emphasizing security and control: "We should take seriously the various types of inherent and secondary risks that AI may trigger. We should put in place laws and regulations, technological monitoring, early warning and emergency response systems in order to strengthen the line of security, prevent abuses and malicious use, and ensure that AI is always under human control." Xi also called for international AI cooperation through the newly launched World Artificial Intelligence Cooperation Organization (WAICO), which includes 29 members including Russia, Pakistan, Brazil, and South Africa. [theregister.com](https://www.theregister.com/ai-and-ml/2026/07/20/chinese-president-xi-jinping-wants-emergency-response-systems-to-keep-ai-in-check/5274687)

### **First Autonomous AI Agent Intrusion Campaign Breaches Hugging Face**

Hugging Face disclosed that an autonomous AI agent breached part of its production infrastructure last week, accessing internal datasets and service credentials. The company detected and contained the intrusion, finding no evidence that attackers modified public AI models, datasets, Spaces, or the software supply chain. Notably, the operation was driven by an autonomous AI agent framework executing thousands of actions across short-lived sandboxes, using public services for self-migrating command-and-control. This represents the first major autonomous agent-driven intrusion campaign, reflecting the rise of AI-powered attacks. Hugging Face has closed vulnerabilities, revoked compromised credentials, and strengthened security controls. [securityaffairs.com](https://securityaffairs.com/195658/ai/ai-agents-turned-into-attackers-hugging-face-reveals-autonomous-intrusion-campaign.html)
